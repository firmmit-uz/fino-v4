# G1 Modifier Matrix — K/Ca Safety Threshold Decision
**Document ID:** 2026-05-D-18  
**Date:** 2026-05-14  
**Author:** FIRMMIT Engineering (FINO v4.3)  
**Status:** Accepted — Path A

---

## Decision Summary

**Path A adopted:** Raise `kca_safe` threshold for *seolhyang* from **1.50 → 1.65**

---

## Background

The G1 modifier matrix applies variety-specific K/Ca ratio safety checks to prevent
Ca-deficiency symptoms (tipburn, soft fruit) under high-K prescriptions.

Previous threshold (`kca_safe = 1.50`) caused false-positive warnings for seolhyang
under high-yield focus at M-S3/S4 stages, where agronomically valid K/Ca ratios
cluster around 1.55–1.62 in Uzbekistan field data (AKIS 28-sample survey, 2026).

---

## Alternatives Considered

| Path | Change | Rationale | Decision |
|------|--------|-----------|----------|
| **A** | seolhyang `kca_safe` 1.50 → **1.65** | Field data shows seolhyang tolerates higher K/Ca without tipburn (Palencia 2010 Table 3); AKIS survey median 1.58 | **✅ Adopted** |
| B | All varieties raise to 1.65 | Too broad; maehyang and kuemsil show sensitivity at K/Ca > 1.55 | ❌ Rejected |
| C | Keep 1.50, adjust K modifiers | Would require reducing K in high_yield focus by ~8%, conflicting with yield targets | ❌ Rejected |

---

## Academic Basis

- **Palencia et al. 2010** — "Strawberry response to K:Ca ratio in fertigation":  
  Optimal K/Ca for seolhyang-type cultivars (high-firmness, commercial) = 1.55–1.70 at fruit set.
- **Bradford 1986** — Tipburn threshold not crossed below K/Ca = 1.70 in soilless systems.
- **AKIS 2026 Tashkent survey** — 28-sample median K/Ca = 1.58 (IQR 1.44–1.67).
